US govt says Americans lost record $12.5 billion to fraud in 2024

The U.S. Federal Trade Commission (FTC) reported that Americans lost a record $12.5 billion to fraud in 2024, a 25% increase over the previous year. Investment scams caused the highest losses, followed by imposter scams.

Key Insights:

  • Investment scams resulted in the largest financial losses.
  • Imposter scams were the second most costly.
  • Younger individuals are increasingly targeted by fraud.

Analyst Notes: The dramatic increase in fraud losses highlights the evolving tactics of cybercriminals. Organizations should emphasize user education on recognizing and avoiding scams, and reinforce multifactor authentication.

Risk Rating: High

Undocumented commands found in Bluetooth chip used by a billion devices

Researchers have discovered undocumented commands in the ESP32 microchip, a Bluetooth chip used in over a billion devices. These commands could be exploited for attacks, including device spoofing and unauthorized data access.

Key Insights:

  • The ESP32 chip is widely used in numerous devices.
  • Undocumented commands pose a significant security risk.
  • Exploitation could lead to device spoofing and data breaches.

Analyst Notes: The discovery of these vulnerabilities underscores the importance of supply chain security. Manufacturers and users of devices containing the ESP32 chip should monitor for updates and mitigations.

Risk Rating: Very High

YouTubers extorted via copyright strikes to spread malware

Cybercriminals are extorting YouTubers by sending bogus copyright claims. They coerce them into promoting malware and cryptocurrency miners on their videos.

Key Insights:

  • Cybercriminals are abusing copyright claim systems.
  • This tactic is used to spread malware and cryptocurrency miners.
  • YouTubers are being extorted into spreading the malware.

Analyst Notes: This attack vector shows how threat actors are adapting social engineering tactics to exploit popular platforms. Content creators should be cautious of copyright claims and verify their legitimacy.

Risk Rating: Medium

US seizes $23 million in crypto linked to LastPass breaches

U.S. authorities have seized over $23 million in cryptocurrency linked to the theft of $150 million from a Ripple crypto wallet in January 2024. Investigators believe that hackers who breached LastPass in 2022 were behind the attack.

Key Insights:

  • Authorities have seized crypto linked to the LastPass breaches.
  • The LastPass breaches are connected to a large crypto theft.
  • This highlights the long lasting effects of data breaches.

Analyst Notes: This demonstrates the long-term consequences of data breaches and the increasing focus on cryptocurrency in cybercrime. Organizations must prioritize strong password management and security.
